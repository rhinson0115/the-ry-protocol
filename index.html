Immediately post-lift while still warm. Moderate, fully conversational pace. Heart rate roughly 60–70% max — nose-breathable. Fat-burn focused, not a performance session.

Aerobic base builder. Easy pace only — this should feel almost too easy. Builds the aerobic engine, accelerates weekly recovery, and creates passive caloric burn without taxing the system for Monday.

Disruption Protocol: If Sunday run gets compromised, accept the miss. Do not compensate by doubling Monday. Reset the week cleanly.

The 6 Rules

No tracking. Follow these in order. They mechanically create a deficit.

1
Plate Architecture — Every Meal ½ plate vegetables or salad first. ¼ plate protein. ¼ plate carbs/starch. Home or restaurant — no exceptions. Eat in that order.
2
Protein Anchors Everything No meal or snack is carbs-only. Every eating occasion has a protein source. Non-negotiable.
3
Eat Protein + Veg First Before touching the carb portion, finish protein and vegetables. Satiety kicks in before you overeat the high-calorie portion.
4
Restaurant Rule Box half before you eat, or split an entrée. Restaurant portions are 2–3x normal. Highest-risk scenario — plan for it.
5
Snacks = Protein + Fiber Only No naked carb snacks. Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, hard boiled eggs, deli roll-ups, edamame. If it's just fruit or crackers — add protein or skip it.
6
Kitchen Closes 2 Hours Before Bed Nothing after that window. Water only. This eliminates your highest-calorie, lowest-quality eating.
Daily Targets: 140–160g protein  •  35–40g fiber  •  1 gallon water  •  8,000–10,000 steps  •  Kitchen closed 2 hrs before bed

Pre-Workout Fuel (60–90 min before)

Rice Cakes + Almond Butter + Hard Boiled Egg: 42g carbs, 14g protein — Rule 2 compliant
Overnight Oats + Protein Powder: 40g carbs, 25g protein — batch prep Sunday
Apple + Cottage Cheese (½ cup): 30g carbs, 14g protein — fast, portable
Greek Yogurt + Berries: 35g carbs, 17g protein — no prep required

Post-Workout Recovery (within 60 min)

Protein Shake + Banana: 35g carbs, 25g protein — fast window closer
Chicken + Sweet Potato + Side Salad: 40g carbs, 35g protein — Rule 1 compliant full meal
Salmon + Brown Rice + Roasted Broccoli: 38g carbs, 34g protein — seafood-forward option
Greek Yogurt + Berries + Protein mixed in: 30g carbs, 30g protein — no cooking

Progressive Overload Strategy

4-Week Cycle:
Week 1: Find working weight  •  Week 2: +1 rep/set  •  Week 3: +1 more rep  •  Week 4: Deload (−20%)  •  Week 5: +5–10 lbs, restart
Example — Hip Thrusts:
W1: 135×4×10    W2: 135×4×11    W3: 135×4×12    W4: 115×4×10    W5: 145×4×10

Running Progression

Phase 1 — Weeks 1–4: Establish the Base
Build to 115 min/week consistently. Tuesday intervals at a controlled pace. Priority is form and aerobic adaptation, not speed.

Phase 2 — Weeks 5–8: Add Volume
Extend Tuesday run to 45 min. Add one interval rep (7×400m). Sunday easy run extends to 35 min. Thursday and Saturday stay the same.

Phase 3 — Weeks 9–12: Add Intensity
Tuesday tempo block extends to 25 min. Consider extending Thursday run to 30 min if recovery metrics allow. Monitor Oura HRV before committing.

Strength Milestones

Month 3: Hip Thrusts +30–40 lbs, OHP +15–20 lbs, visible glute growth, noticeable body composition shift from combined training + nutrition
Month 6: Hip Thrusts +60–80 lbs, significant transformation
Month 9–10: Hip Thrusts 225–255 lbs — SNATCHED ACHIEVED
Muay Thai Flag: When advancing to higher-intensity classes, reassess Tuesday run volume — the back-to-back demand increases significantly.
